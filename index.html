Stephanie Su

San Francisco Bay Area

Hi there! I'm Stephanie, a 17-year-old interested in tech, business & social impact. I aim to create value for others through non-profit leadership, pro-bono consultations, and public speaking. In the future, I hope to pursue a degree in Computer Science, and a career in product management and social entrepreneurship.

Growing up near the Silicon Valley, I've always been exposed to STEM fields and developed interests in math and technology early on. However, I was often a gender minority in STEM classes and competitions, which led me to believe that women simply "weren't interested" in STEM. After attending Superposition II, my first all-women hackathon, I discovered that there are so many girls who are interested in STEM, but may not have the opportunities nor support needed to succeed. Now, as Lead Director of Superposition, I help introduce more girls into STEM through hackathons, mentorship, workshops, and chapters. My work with Superposition has motivated me to continue creating social impact through other organizations, such as Ad Astra Youth and Fiveable.

In my free time, I love trying Bay Area boba shops, experimenting with makeup, and shopping second-hand! Currently, I'm watching Avatar: The Last Airbender for the 5th time. I'm always eager to meet new people, so feel free to reach out for a chat!

Ad Astra Youth

Founder & Executive Director (2020 - present)

Ad Astra Youth empowers student-led initiatives to create social impact through pro-bono consultations & mentorship. I founded Ad Astra after having coffee chats with student and discovering that many were eager to create impact, but didn't know where to start. By sharing my knowledge, I help student leaders feel more confident in themselves and their initiatives.

• Consulted 105 student-run nonprofits & businesses in 7 countries about operations, team management, event planning, marketing, branding, expansion
• Launched month-long accelerator program for 12 mentee nonprofits
• Guided mentees in organizing 3 virtual conferences with 2000 attendees in 69 countries
• Managed all social media platforms, marketing, content creation (@adastrayouth)
